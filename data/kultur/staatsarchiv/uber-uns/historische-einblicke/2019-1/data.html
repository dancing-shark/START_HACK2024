    Switzerland as a tourist destination

    Swiss families commonly enjoy one or more holidays between June and October. Of course, one option is to holiday in Switzerland. This is a hot topic these days, especially in view of the climate debate. Old travel guides and articles offer an interesting way to (re)discover regions on our doorstep. When viewed through the eyes of visitors from the late 18th[1] and particularly the 19th century, landscapes and cities can have a special charm, offering new perspectives on the places we think we know so well. In many cases, these old articles are not just about mountains or buildings. They are also about popular culture, politics, economics, history and much more. Switzerland was seen as an almost exotic destination for early travellers. Their reports often include very personal experiences. If the traveller was a writer or poet, the villages, lakes and mountains they visited found their way into their novels or poems. An Alpine trip, for example, inspired the Polish poet Slowacki to write the poem entitled W Szwajcarii (In Switzerland), published in 1839.
